## The Library is now a recordings manager

The Library used to be a simple list. It's now a full table of everything
you've recorded or streamed:

- **Thumbnails** for every recording, generated automatically — including
  your older sessions.
- **The truth about every file**: real size on disk, duration, format, and
  the scene it was recorded with.
- **Search, filter, and sort** — find a recording by name, show only
  recordings or only streams, newest or oldest first.
- **Rename** a session, **duplicate** it, or **import** a video from
  anywhere on your Mac (MP4, MOV, MKV, WebM) into your library.
- **Safe delete**: deleting a recording moves the file to the system Trash —
  the Trash is your undo. Videorc never erases a recording outright. Select
  several rows to clean up in one go.
- An honest **storage footer**: how much space your recordings actually use
  and how much free disk you have left. No made-up quotas.

The title bar and toolbar stay put while you scroll, and the Publish page
shows the same thumbnails and scene names, so both pages read as one system.

## Live comments, upgraded

The Comments window is now a true multi-platform chat reader:

- Comments from **every platform you're live on** arrive in one feed, each
  one tagged with its platform icon and the commenter's avatar.
- **Click a comment to feature it on the stream** — the username, avatar,
  and message appear on your video for everyone watching. Click again to
  take it off.
- **Reply once, everywhere**: write a message in the Comments window and
  Videorc posts it to every platform that supports it.

Twitch note: sending chat messages needs a fresh sign-in once, since Twitch
requires an extra permission — Videorc will ask when it's needed.

## Black glass

The dark theme has been retuned to Videorc's logo: a true black-glass base
with polished edges and the LED-red accent reserved for record and live.
Light mode gets the same treatment as a clean porcelain twin. Same layout,
same controls — just a deeper, calmer finish.
